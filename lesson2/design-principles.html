Visual Hierarchy

Word Reference

wordreference.com

Visual Hierarchy is the principle of arranging elements to show their order of importance. On the mobile version of the Word Reference website you see that the most important (and useful) part of the website is the search bar. Following that is the language to language drop down menu, and lastly the individual language dictionaries at the bottom.

Fitt's Law

Simply Chocolate

simplechocolatecph.com

Fitt's law states that that the time required to move to a target area is a function of the distance to the target and the size of the distance to the target and the size of the target. Basically, it takes less time for a mouse pointer to click a button or link if that button or link is big. On the Simply Chocolate website we see that a tempting chocolate bar occupies a lot of space on the screen and directly over it is a big "shop" square that invites a finger to tap on it.

Hick's Law

Stock X

stockx.com

Hick's law states that the more choices you are given, the longer it takes you to make a decision. This is true for all website visitors. An easy way to manages the many choices a website can offer its customers is through filters. On the mobile website for StockX we see here that you can apply specific filters to find a specific type of clothing, whether its sneakers, appearel or accessories.
